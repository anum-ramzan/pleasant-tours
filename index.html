Culture of Sindh
The Culture of Sindh has its roots in the Indus Valley Civilization. Sindh has been shaped by the geography of the largely desert region, the natural resources it had available and the continuous foreign influences.Sindhi culture is also practiced by the Sindhi diaspora.
Learn more about Sindhi Culture
Culture of Punjab
The culture of Punjab derives its basis from the institution of Sufi saints, who spread Islam and preached and lived the Muslim way of life. People have festivities to commemorate these traditions. The fairs and festivals of Punjab reflect the entire gamut of its folk life and cultural traditions
Learn more about Punjabi Culture
Culture of Balochistan
Though Balochistan is an area of barren lands, deserts and mountains, the Culture of Balochistan is full of traditions, arts and crafts. Balochi embroidery is one of the most popular arts and crafts which are done by the females. Another distinct feature of Baloch culture is the storytelling tradition.
Learn more about Balochi Culture
Culture of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa
Pashtun culture is based on Islam and Pashtunwali, which is an ancient way of life, as well as speaking of the Pashto language and wearing Pashtun dress. The Pashtun culture has little outside influence and, over the ages, has retained a great degree of purity.
Learn more about Pashtun Culture
